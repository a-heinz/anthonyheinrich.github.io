Hi there!

My name is Anthony Heinrich and I am a Canadian artist currently based in Montreal, having spent time living in Ottawa, Toronto, and Amsterdam. I work with pens and watercolour and my focus on the moment is documenting different cities I’ve been fortunate enough to visit over the past five years.

My work is done freehand without use of a ruler. I tend to use a grid system to first establish the structure of the landscape by pencil. Once the most basic skeleton of the drawing has been set, I pick a spot (usually at the bottom of the page) and get the ink flowing. Choosing this first spot is undoubtedly the hardest part of the whole process. Pen is permanent - there’s no going back once the first line hits the page. And while this notion begets some hesitation from me at first, once I do start drawing I enter an unwavering state of flow that is very difficult to break from.

Cities are complex, busy, rich, cacophonous, and metamorphic. And depending on where you are, be it high up on a rooftop or emerging from within the depths of a metro tunnel, your perspective of these urban jungles is constantly changing with each step you take. The next time you take a walk down a city block, stop for a second and look up at the structures around you. Notice the cat basking in that 2nd floor window sill, the tree branch bending precariously over an alleyway balcony, and the woman peeling the faded “For Rent” sign off her attic window. The overlooked, the puzzle pieces, the nitty-gritty - these details are what I try to capture with my drawings.
